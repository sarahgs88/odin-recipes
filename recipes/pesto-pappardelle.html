Pesto Pappardelle

Description

An ode to basil. This recipe lets the pappardelle ribons swim in a fresh pesto sauce and adds some crunch with toasted breadcrumbs.


Ingredients

  • 1 egg pasta dough
  • 1 bunch of fresh basil
  • 4 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 lemon
  • Pine nuts
  • Toasted breadcrumbs
  • Red pepper flakes
  • Parmesean cheese
  • Kosher Salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Steps

To make the pesto
  1. Add the basil, pine nuts, parmesean, salt, and pepper to a food processor.
  2. Blend while drizzling in the olive oil.
  3. Add additional oil until it reaches desired consistency
  4. Store the sauce for later.
Assemble the dish
  1. Roll out the pasta dough to the thinness of 4 post-it notes.
  2. Flour the dough and fold the pasta sheet onto itself a few inches at a time, almost like rolling up a carpet.
  3. Cut the rolled pasta sheet into 1 inch sections and unravel the pappardelle ribbons.
  4. Set a pot of water to boil and season generously with salt.
  5. Cook the pasta in the boiling water until tender (1-2 minutes). Reserve some of the pasta water.
  6. Add the pesto and pasta water to the skillet pan on low heat. Once done, add the pasta
  7. Plate the pesto and top with red pepper flakes, parmesean, fresh basil, and breadcrumbs.
